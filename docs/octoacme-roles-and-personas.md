# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Project Sponsor

### Role Summary
The Project Sponsor provides strategic direction, secures funding, and champions the project at the executive level. They serve as the ultimate decision-maker for scope, budget, and priority escalations.

### Responsibilities
- Provide strategic direction and business justification for the project
- Secure and allocate funding and resources
- Champion the project at the executive level
- Make final decisions on scope changes and priority conflicts
- Remove organizational blockers escalated by the Project Manager
- Ensure alignment with overall business objectives

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers expected business value
- Maintain executive visibility and support
- Enable timely decision-making for escalated issues

### Typical Communication
- Monthly executive briefings and status reviews
- Ad-hoc escalation meetings with Project Manager
- Approval gates at major milestones

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager:** Receives regular status updates, provides direction on escalations, approves major scope changes
- **Product Manager:** Aligns on business priorities and success metrics
- **Stakeholders:** Communicates project value and secures cross-functional support

---

## Change Control Lead

### Role Summary
The Change Control Lead owns change management processes by reviewing, approving, and documenting changes in scope, requirements, or schedule. They ensure all changes are properly assessed for impact before implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Review and assess all proposed changes to project scope, requirements, or schedule
- Document change requests and maintain the change log
- Coordinate impact assessments with affected teams
- Approve or escalate changes based on defined thresholds
- Ensure changes follow the established change control process
- Communicate approved changes to relevant stakeholders

### Goals
- Minimize uncontrolled scope creep
- Ensure all changes are properly documented and assessed
- Maintain project stability while allowing necessary adaptations

### Typical Communication
- Change request reviews and approval meetings
- Impact assessment discussions with technical leads
- Weekly change log summaries to Project Manager

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager:** Collaborates on change prioritization, reports on change request status
- **Developers:** Coordinates impact assessments for technical changes
- **Product Manager:** Reviews changes affecting product scope or priorities
- **Project Sponsor:** Escalates high-impact changes for approval

---

## Communications Coordinator

### Role Summary
The Communications Coordinator ensures regular, effective communication across teams and with external stakeholders. They manage project updates, information distribution schedules, and maintain communication channels.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain the project communication plan
- Coordinate regular status updates and stakeholder briefings
- Manage internal and external communication channels
- Ensure timely distribution of project information
- Facilitate cross-team communication and information sharing
- Maintain project documentation accessibility

### Goals
- Ensure all stakeholders receive timely and relevant information
- Reduce communication gaps and misunderstandings
- Maintain consistent messaging across all channels

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status reports and newsletters
- Stakeholder update meetings coordination
- Release announcements and milestone communications

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Manager:** Coordinates on status updates and stakeholder messaging
- **Product Manager:** Aligns on product announcements and feature communications
- **Stakeholders:** Manages expectations and information distribution
- **Project Sponsor:** Prepares executive communications and briefings

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
The Quality Assurance Lead is responsible for maintaining process standards and verifying deliverables against requirements. They ensure quality gates are met and lead retrospectives for continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain quality standards and processes
- Review deliverables against acceptance criteria and requirements
- Lead quality gate reviews at key milestones
- Coordinate testing strategies with development teams
- Facilitate retrospectives and capture lessons learned
- Track and report on quality metrics

### Goals
- Ensure deliverables meet defined quality standards
- Drive continuous process improvement
- Reduce defects and rework through proactive quality practices

### Typical Communication
- Quality gate reviews and sign-offs
- Test planning and coordination meetings
- Retrospective facilitation and action item tracking

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers:** Collaborates on testing strategies and quality standards
- **Project Manager:** Reports on quality metrics and gate status
- **Product Manager:** Ensures acceptance criteria are testable and met
- **Change Control Lead:** Validates changes meet quality requirements before approval
